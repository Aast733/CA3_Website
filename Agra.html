Agra

The city of love

Agra, a city in northern India, is renowned for its historical significance and stunning architecture. Located on the banks of the Yamuna River, it was once the capital of the Mughal Empire. Agra is best known for the Taj Mahal, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the Seven Wonders of the World. This magnificent mausoleum was built by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal. The Taj Mahal’s white marble façade, intricate carvings, and exquisite gardens attract millions of visitors each year, making it a symbol of love and a masterpiece of Mughal architecture. Apart from the Taj, Agra is home to the Agra Fort, another UNESCO World Heritage Site. This imposing red sandstone fortress served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors until 1638 and showcases a blend of Islamic and Hindu architectural styles. The city is also known for its rich culture, vibrant bazaars, and delectable cuisine. Visitors can explore local markets, where artisans sell intricate marble inlay work, leather goods, and traditional handicrafts. Agra's culinary scene offers a taste of Mughlai flavors, with dishes like biryani, kebabs, and the famous petha, a sweet made from ash gourd.

Restaurants

Flavourful Journey

Peshawri hotel

The Peshawri restaurant in Agra, part of the celebrated ITC Mughal hotel, is renowned for its distinctive North-West Frontier cuisine, which draws on traditional flavors and cooking methods from the region. Inspired by the rugged simplicity of the cuisine along the Indian frontier, Peshawri offers diners a taste of authentic, rustic dishes served in a warm, earthy ambiance. The restaurant’s signature open kitchen concept lets diners watch skilled chefs prepare the food over charcoal flames, in the tandoor ovens, and on live grills, evoking the vibrant culinary traditions of the region.

Taj Mahal hotel

The Taj Mahal Palace Hotel in Mumbai, India, is one of the most iconic and historic luxury hotels in the world. Established in 1903 by Jamsetji Tata, it was the first harbor landmark in the city and is noted for its stunning blend of Islamic and Renaissance architectural styles. Overlooking the Arabian Sea and adjacent to the Gateway of India, the Taj Mahal Palace is synonymous with Mumbai’s heritage and grandeur.

sankalp restaurant

Sankalp Restaurant in Agra is a popular dining spot known for its authentic South Indian cuisine. Located in the bustling heart of the city, this restaurant offers a welcoming ambiance with traditional decor, perfect for family gatherings and casual outings. Sankalp’s menu boasts a variety of South Indian dishes, from crispy dosas and soft idlis to tangy sambars and spicy chutneys, capturing the essence of southern flavors

Pind baluchi

Pind Balluchi in Agra is a popular dining spot that brings the charm of rural Punjab to the heart of the city. Known for its vibrant ambiance and traditional decor, this restaurant is designed to replicate a rustic Punjabi village setting. The interior is adorned with vintage artifacts, charpais, clay pots, and traditional Punjabi motifs, creating an immersive cultural experience.
