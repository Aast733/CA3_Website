Amritsar

The golden City

Amritsar, a vibrant city in Punjab, India, is renowned for its rich cultural heritage and historical significance. Founded in the 16th century, it is home to the Golden Temple, the holiest shrine of Sikhism, attracting millions of pilgrims and tourists alike. The temple’s golden façade glistens against the serene waters of the Amrit Sarovar, adding to its beauty and tranquility. The city’s bustling streets are filled with the aroma of traditional Punjabi cuisine, including mouthwatering dishes like Amritsari kulcha, lassi, and chole bhature. Amritsar is also famous for its street food, where vendors serve up delectable snacks like jalebi, samosas, and golgappas. Amritsar’s history is deeply intertwined with the struggles and sacrifices of the Sikh community, most notably marked by the tragic events of the Jallianwala Bagh massacre in 1919. This memorial site stands as a testament to the resilience of the people and is a poignant reminder of the fight for freedom.

Flavourful Journey

Ramada

Ramada Hotel in Amritsar offers a blend of modern amenities and traditional charm, conveniently located near the Golden Temple. With elegant rooms, a rooftop pool, fine dining, and warm hospitality, it’s ideal for both leisure and business travelers.

Kesar Dhaba

Amritsar’s Kesar Da Dhaba is a historic vegetarian eatery, famous for its rich, authentic Punjabi flavors. Established in 1916, it offers classics like dal makhani, lachha parathas, and creamy lassis, all served in a rustic, cozy setting.

Haveli

A haveli in Amritsar is a traditional mansion that reflects the rich cultural heritage of Punjab. With intricate carvings, vibrant frescoes, and grand courtyards, these havelis blend Mughal and Sikh architecture, offering a glimpse into Amritsar’s historic charm.
